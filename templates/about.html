About

The Cryo-electron microscopy facility at IISc features a state-of-the-art 200 kV Talos-Arctica electron microscope (FEI/Thermo Scientific) equipped with a Gatan K2 Summit Direct Electron Detector, automatic data acquisition via the Latitude S software package, and a T12 BioTwin Transmission Electron Microscope. Typically, the T12 BioTwin is used for negative stain sample visualization before proceeding to cryo-EM data collection using the 200 kV Talos-Arctica microscope.

The facility is also equipped with essential sample preparation tools such as the FEI Vitrobotâ„¢ Mark IV, GloQube glow discharge system, and Q150T ES carbon coater.

We provide one of the finest cryo-EM facilities in India, supporting negative staining, cryo-EM sample preparation, high-throughput data acquisition, fast data transfer, secure storage, and automatic movie frame alignment. Researchers are supported throughout sample preparation, data collection, and preliminary image analysis.

Mission: Our goal is to offer open access to the cryo-EM facility for all researchers through a nominal charge or collaborative model. Renowned faculty, postdocs, and EM managers at IISc provide training on protein purification, sample prep, data collection, and basic image processing. Our broader vision is to establish a national-level cryo-EM hub that benefits researchers across India.

Note: Currently, full-scale data processing support is limited to collaborative projects only.

This facility is supported by the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India, through Infrastructure Grants, and by the DST-FIST grant secured by the Molecular Biophysics Unit, IISc.
